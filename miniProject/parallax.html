Online gaming is simply the playing of a video game over the internet, usually with friends. Online games can be played on any number of devices from dedicated video games consoles such as PlayStations, Xboxes, and Nintendo Switches, to PCs, laptops and mobile phones.

Valorant is a popular tactical first-person shooter (FPS) game developed and published by Riot Games. Released in 2020, Valorant has gained widespread acclaim for its combination of precise gunplay and unique character abilities. The game features a diverse cast of characters called "Agents," each with their own distinct abilities that players can use strategically during matches. Valorant's gameplay emphasizes teamwork, strategy, and precise shooting skills. It incorporates elements of traditional FPS games with the addition of special abilities that add a layer of complexity to the gameplay. The objective-based game modes include attacking and defending bomb sites, similar to classic game modes in the genre.

The competitive nature of Valorant has led to the development of a dedicated esports scene, with professional tournaments and leagues attracting a large audience. The game continues to receive updates and new content, keeping the player base engaged and contributing to its ongoing success in the competitive gaming community.

PlayerUnknown's Battlegrounds (PUBG) is a highly popular battle royale game developed and published by PUBG Corporation, a subsidiary of South Korean video game company Bluehole. It was officially released in March 2017 and quickly became a global phenomenon. PUBG is credited with popularizing the battle royale genre, where a large number of players, often up to 100, compete against each other until only one player or team remains. The game is set on a fictional island where players parachute onto the map and scavenge for weapons, equipment, and resources to survive. As the match progresses, a shrinking play zone forces players into closer proximity, intensifying the encounters and promoting strategic gameplay. The last player or team standing wins the match.

PUBG's success lies in its realistic graphics, immersive gameplay, and a focus on strategic decision-making. It has been praised for its tense and unpredictable matches, offering a unique blend of exploration, survival, and combat. PUBG has a significant esports presence, with numerous tournaments and competitions held globally. The game has also inspired a variety of spin-offs and adaptations across different platforms.

Free Fire is a popular battle royale game developed by 111 Dots Studio and published by Garena. Launched in 2017, Free Fire quickly gained immense popularity, particularly in regions such as Southeast Asia, South America, and India. It is designed for mobile devices but can also be played on PC through emulators. The game follows the battle royale format, where 50 players parachute onto an island and compete to be the last person or team standing. Free Fire distinguishes itself with shorter match durations, typically lasting around 10 minutes, making it more accessible for players with limited time.

Free Fire features a diverse cast of characters, each with unique abilities. Players can choose their starting point, collect weapons, and engage in intense gunfights within a shrinking play zone. The game offers various modes, including solo, duo, and squad matches, as well as events and limited-time game modes. In addition to its battle royale mode, Free Fire includes other features such as character customization, weapon skins, and in-game purchases. The game has a significant esports scene with tournaments and leagues, contributing to its sustained popularity among mobile gamers globally.

Minecraft is a sandbox video game that was created and designed by Markus "Notch" Persson and later developed and published by Mojang Studios. Released in 2011, Minecraft has become one of the best-selling video games of all time, known for its open-world exploration, creativity, and limitless possibilities.

Key features of Minecraft include: Open-world Sandbox: Minecraft offers a vast, procedurally generated world made up of blocks, each representing various materials and environments. Players can explore, gather resources, and shape the world as they see fit. Creative Mode: In Creative mode, players have unlimited resources and can build freely without worrying about survival elements. This mode is popular for creative and artistic endeavors. Survival Mode: In Survival mode, players must gather resources, craft tools, and survive against various threats such as hostile mobs and environmental dangers. Players can also engage in farming, mining, and other activities to sustain themselves. Multiplayer: Minecraft supports multiplayer gameplay, allowing players to join servers or play with friends in shared worlds. This collaborative aspect has led to the creation of vast multiplayer communities and servers with unique gameplay experiences.
